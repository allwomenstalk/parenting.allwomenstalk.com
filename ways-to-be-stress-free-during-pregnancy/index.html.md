# 10 Proven Ways to Be Stress\-Free During Pregnancy

Pregnancy is a significant demand on a woman’s body, so knowing ways to be stress-free during pregnancy is incredibly helpful. Hormone levels are often in flux for most expectant mothers, and if you add stress to the mix, you may find yourself feeling beyond overwhelmed. It's essential to prioritize your mental health just as much as your physical health during this transformative time. Here are my best tips and ways to stay stress-free during pregnancy.

## 1. Plan Ahead

One of the absolute best ways to be stress-free during pregnancy is to plan ahead. While you can’t realistically plan for everything, as many aspects are out of your control, having a foundation can help. You can have things set into place to be [prepared for the baby](https://parenting.allwomenstalk.com/ways-to-prepare-for-a-new-baby/) and have backup arrangements made in case things don't go exactly as you had planned.

## 2. Do Your Research

A great way to avoid stressing out while you are pregnant is to know what to expect. If pregnancy is a new experience for you, it's natural to worry about every new change or symptom. The [Mayo Clinic](https://www.mayoclinic.org/healthy-lifestyle/pregnancy-week-by-week/in-depth/pregnancy/art-20047232) offers excellent resources on what is normal and when to consult a professional. Do your research so you will know what is cause for worry and what is just part of the journey!

## 3. Have Some Support on Hand

Your number one method for staying stress-free when pregnant is to have support close at hand. Whether it’s your spouse, your mother, a friend, or a coworker, you need someone who can be there for you and understand your worries and fears. Join a local support group for expectant mothers if possible, as connecting with others in the same situation can be incredibly grounding.

## 4. Bond with Your Baby

Bonding with your unborn baby will be one of the most amazing experiences you can imagine. It calms and relaxes both of you, and stimulates a connection between the baby and the world. Talk to your child and rub your belly daily to help the bonding process. This simple act of connection can significantly lower your cortisol levels.

## 5. Pamper Yourself

Nothing helps de-stress a mom-to-be like some well-deserved pampering! Every now and then, go get a pedicure or a facial. Consider a [pre-natal massage](https://americanpregnancy.org/healthy-pregnancy/pregnancy-health-wellness/prenatal-massage/) at least once while expecting; you'll be amazed by how much it can reduce your stress levels.

## 6. Avoid Overdoing It

When pregnant women "overdo" it, their stress levels are likely to spike. Avoid committing to events that feel like too much to handle. Don’t put unnecessary pressure on yourself! Try to keep your personal activities, such as shopping, doctor appointments, and other errands, spaced out over the week so you can still relax a bit each day.

**Quick Daily De-stress Checklist:**

- Take three deep breaths every hour.
- Drink a glass of water before every meal.
- Spend 5 minutes in quiet meditation.
- Say one positive affirmation about your pregnancy.

## 7. Eat Healthy Foods

Good, nutritious food is where overall health starts, including your emotional well-being. If you’re feeling constantly stressed, and it’s not due to your environment or personal issues, try switching up your diet. Eating fresh fruits, vegetables, and lean protein can have a huge impact on your emotions almost overnight!

| Food Group | Key Benefit | Example |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Leafy Greens | Folate for baby's development | Spinach, Kale |
| Healthy Fats | Brain health and mood stability | Avocado, Walnuts |
| Complex Carbs | Sustained energy levels | Quinoa, Sweet Potatoes |

## 8. Get Plenty of Rest

Nurturing a baby is a lot of work, and moms need their rest! If you have a spare minute or two in your day, take a nap. It will be good for both you and the baby, and you will lower your stress levels significantly just by catching up on some sleep.

## 9. Surround Yourself with Positive People

Negative attitudes can really affect a mother-to-be. Surround yourself with positive people who help soothe and relax you. Avoid situations where tension and strife may arise to keep the environment around you and your baby as calm as possible!

## 10. Take Things One Day at a Time

Many times, the best way to approach pregnancy is to take it one day at a time. It's easy to get overwhelmed when you start thinking about everything that needs to be done. Pregnancy is a wonderful time that we don’t experience every day, so focus on the present and let the future take care of itself. [Healthy habits](https://parenting.allwomenstalk.com/healthy-habits-to-start-during-pregnancy/) started now will benefit you for years to come, especially if you take the time to [plan ahead](#1).

These are my tips for a stress-free pregnancy, but I'd love to hear from you. Do you have any ways to stay calm while expecting? I’d love to hear your thoughts, so please comment below. Thanks for reading!
