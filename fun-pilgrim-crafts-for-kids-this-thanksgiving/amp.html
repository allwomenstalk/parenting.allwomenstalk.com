3 Paper Bag Tepees

Source: Art with Mr. Giannetto
Chances are you have some brown paper lunch bags at home. Use them to make these teepees that the pilgrims may have lived in at one time.

***

Paper Bag Tepees are a great craft for kids to make this Thanksgiving. This craft is an excellent way to teach kids about the pilgrims and the kind of dwellings they lived in. All you need is some brown paper lunch bags and a few other materials. First, cut the bottom off the paper bags. Then, fold the bag in half and staple it together. Next, cut a 1-inch slit in the center of the bag and use it to join the two sides together. Finally, use glue to attach the top and bottom of the bag together.

This craft is easy to make and requires minimal materials. Kids will love decorating their tepees with markers, crayons, feathers, and other materials. This craft is also a great way to help kids understand the history of the pilgrims and their lifestyle. Not only will kids have fun making the tepees, but they will also learn something new about the pilgrims. This craft is a great way to get kids excited about Thanksgiving and its history.
