# 7 Surprising Pregnancy Facts You Need to Know

Pregnancy is an incredible journey, and while many people understand the basics of conception, there is so much more happening beneath the surface. Beyond the miracle of growing a beautiful baby, there are plenty of surprising physical and emotional changes that catch expectant parents off guard. If you are wondering about the lesser-known side effects or simply want to be fully prepared, you are not alone. I have compiled 7 pregnancy facts you should know, covering everything from mysterious cravings to unexpected symptoms.

## 1. All That Weight Gained Isn't Fat

It is completely normal to see the scale go up, so try not to stress over every pound. Believe it or not, a significant portion of your weight gain has absolutely nothing to do with your eating habits. For example, your body produces extra blood to support the baby, which amounts to about 3 pounds. Your growing womb weighs roughly 2 pounds, and the placenta adds another 1.5 pounds.

| Component | Estimated Weight | Purpose |
| --- | --- | --- |
| Extra blood | 3 lbs | Supports oxygen flow to the baby |
| Amniotic fluid | 2 lbs | Protects and cushions the baby |
| Placenta | 1.5 lbs | Delivers nutrients |

On top of that, you can expect about 2 pounds of amniotic fluid, 4 pounds of water retention, and 2 pounds of extra breast tissue. And, of course, let's not forget the weight of the baby itself. Out of all the changes your body goes through, only a fraction of the weight you gain is actually fatty tissue. For official recommendations, you can always check the guidelines from the [American College of Obstetricians and Gynecologists](https://www.acog.org/clinical/clinical-guidance/committee-opinion/articles/2013/01/weight-gain-during-pregnancy).

## 2. The "Glow" Thing is Real

When friends tell you that you are glowing, they aren't just being polite. There is a real biological reason behind this famous pregnancy trait. As a result of your body [producing extra blood](#1), your blood volume actually increases by up to 50% to help nourish your baby. This extra blood flow brings more circulation to your skin. Plus, your oil glands are working a little harder than usual, which naturally gives you a slightly shiny, radiant look.

## 3. Men Can Experience a "Sympathetic Pregnancy"

While the birthing parent goes through the most obvious physical changes, some partners experience what is known as sympathetic pregnancy, or Couvade syndrome. Some men actually report symptoms like bloating, weight gain, indigestion, and morning sickness. They might even develop weird cravings and mood swings.

- Nausea or morning sickness

- Unexpected food cravings

- Unexplained mood swings

It is a fascinating psychological response to the stress and excitement of becoming a parent. While it might not be the most comfortable experience for the dad-to-be, it certainly gives partners a small taste of what pregnancy actually feels like.

## 4. There May Be a Reason You Crave Pickles

Some researchers suggest that when pregnant women crave pickles, their bodies might actually be seeking the preservatives rather than the cucumber itself. Vinegar is believed to help the body break down calcium, making it easier to absorb. Since pregnant women need extra calcium to support their baby's bone development, this craving could be your body's clever way of ensuring it gets exactly what it needs. Alternatively, your body might just be slightly deficient in sodium, and it is seeking the salt found in pickle brine. For more healthy eating tips, check out our guide on [maintaining a balanced pregnancy diet](/healthy-pregnancy-diet/).

## 5. Eating Fish May Be Beneficial While Pregnant

A comprehensive study involving 7,000 women demonstrated that eating fish during pregnancy might boost a baby's brain development and improve communication skills. The research compared women who ate fish once a week to those who avoided it entirely. When their babies were tested at 15 months old, the children of mothers who consumed fish scored higher on verbal skills assessments. The omega-3 fatty acids found in seafood are crucial for neurological growth. You can read more about the benefits of omega-3s in [this clinical review](https://www.ncbi.nlm.nih.gov/pmc/articles/PMC8143493/). Just remember to stick to safe, low-mercury options like salmon or tilapia.

## 6. Hot Months Are Popular

It turns out that the hot months of July, August, and September consistently rank as the most popular times for giving birth. More babies arrive during this late summer window than any other time of the year. If you do the math, it suggests that many couples are staying cozy indoors during the cold winter months. It is a fun statistical quirk that makes late summer a very busy time for maternity wards.

## 7. Woman Can Go All Nine Months without Knowing

Television shows have popularized the concept of women giving birth without ever realizing they were expecting, but this phenomenon happens more frequently than you might assume. Studies conducted in Germany and Ireland have indicated that approximately 1 in every 600 births involves a person who had no idea they were pregnant until labor began. Known as a cryptic pregnancy, it is hard to imagine going a full nine months without noticing the symptoms or feeling the baby move. However, factors like irregular periods and subtle fetal movements make it entirely possible.
