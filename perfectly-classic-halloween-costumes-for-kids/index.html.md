# Want to Save Money on Cheap Halloween Costumes for Kids? Try This

Are you searching for traditional halloween costumes that never go out of style? Every October, children enjoy dressing up and going door-to-door collecting treats. Before the spooky festivities can begin, parents and kids must find the right outfits, but coming up with creative kids costume ideas can feel overwhelming. If your child is anything like mine, the brainstorming for the next holiday starts the moment they finish trick-or-treating. Even when they have plenty of fun ideas, narrowing down the choices to find that perfect fit is always a challenge. To help make your planning a little easier, we have put together this guide to help you find classic kids halloween costumes that are both timeless and budget-friendly. You can also explore our favorite [Halloween traditions to start with your family](https://parenting.allwomenstalk.com/fun-halloween-traditions-to-start-with-your-family/) to make the season even more memorable.

| Costume Idea | DIY Effort | Key Items |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Ghost | Minimal | White sheet, scissors |
| Scarecrow | Low | Plaid shirt, straw, floppy hat |
| Cowboy / Cowgirl | Low | Boots, flannel, hat |

## 1. Ghost

Can you think of a more classic Halloween costume for kids? You can go Charlie Brown style on this and just cut a couple of holes in a white sheet, or look for something more detailed. I personally am a huge fan of the Charlie Brown ghost costumes, which you can read about on the official [Peanuts website](https://www.peanuts.com).

## 2. Witch

Witches are always a Halloween favorite. Your little girl's witch can be gnarled with warts and a large nose or a little sassy with a cute outfit and wonderful makeup. That’s the fun of Halloween; you can put your own spin on classic Halloween costumes for kids.

## 3. Pumpkin

I always find baby pumpkin costumes so adorable. Of course, there are plenty of pumpkin Jack-o-Lantern costumes available for older children too. If you are decorating your porch as well, don't miss our guide on [creative pumpkin carving ideas for beginners](https://parenting.allwomenstalk.com/creative-pumpkin-carving-ideas-for-beginners/). This look is a simple and classic Halloween costume for kids.

## 4. Princess

Do you have a little girl? If you do, I’m sure she already dresses up like a princess a majority of the time. I have a little girl too. Some costumes make me cringe at the prices, but with a princess costume I don’t mind spending the extra money because I know it is going to get worn many more times.

## 5. Scarecrow

Looking for a classic Halloween costume for kids you can make at home? How about a scarecrow? I think a scarecrow costume would be very simple to make. If you enjoy crafting, you might also want to check out some fun [DIY Halloween crafts for kids](https://parenting.allwomenstalk.com/diy-halloween-crafts-for-kids/) to add a personal touch to your home decor. To put this look together, you will need:

- A plaid shirt
- Comfortable jeans or overalls
- A floppy straw hat
- A bit of faux straw or raffia
Add some straw and you are done.

## 6. Clown

Do you like clowns? Adult clowns creep me out a little bit, but little kid clowns are awesome. I love the bright colors, fun clothes, and crazy hair. If you want a classic Halloween costume for kids, try a clown.

## 7. Zombie

Zombies have been quite the craze lately. The idea of zombie costumes has been around for a long time. If you wanted to create this look at home without buying a costume it would be simple. Just rip up some old clothes, make them look dirty, and add some creepy makeup. That is all you need for an amazing zombie costume.

## 8. Vampire

I love vampires. I don’t suggest going the Twilight route and making your little vampire sparkle in the light, but if that is your thing, go for it. My favorite part of this classic Halloween costume for kids is the makeup. Create a perfect vampire face with some interesting contouring, dark eyes, and blood red lips. It's a great option if they want something spooky but a bit more classic than a decaying [zombie](#7).

## 9. Ballerina

Ballerina is another girls' classic Halloween costume. It’s also another easy one to put together, plus your child can wear it over and over again at home. You can easily assemble this look with:

- A matching leotard
- A fluffy tulle tutu
- Soft pink tights and ballet flats
Of course, if your daughter takes dance, you can use one of her dance recital outfits for Halloween. Complete the look by pulling the hair up into a bun and add a tiara if you wish. What could be more precious than that?

## 10. Rock Star

Is your child a natural rock star at heart? If so, why not let that rocker image shine through this Halloween? A little leather jacket, ripped jeans, and spiked hair complete the rock star look.

## 11. Ninja

Who doesn’t want to be a ninja? If I didn’t have to uphold my image of a mature, respectable adult, I’d walk around as a ninja every day. Even better, I’d carry around ninja stars and sling them at people who make me mad. Don’t worry; I wouldn't try to hit them. I just want to shake them up a little. I bet your child would love to be a ninja this Halloween too.

## 12. Super Hero

Chances are your child has a favorite super hero. Fortunately, the costume stores are filled with super hero classic Halloween costumes. Whether they love characters from [Marvel](https://www.marvel.com) or DC, let your child pick out his or her favorite and have a great Halloween.

## 13. Cartoon Character

Just like superheroes, your child probably has a favorite cartoon character as well. SpongeBob, Dora, and Disney characters are just a few popular cartoon characters your child may be interested in dressing up as on Halloween. Find out which one they like best and choose that as this year's Halloween costume. You can find inspiration directly on the official [Disney website](https://www.disney.com).

## 14. Animals

Cats, dogs, cows, ducks, and little chickens all make incredible classic Halloween costumes for children. Did you have a favorite animal you dressed up like on Halloween? I once saw a baby dressed as a skunk for Halloween. Words can't describe how adorable that little stinker was.

## 15. Angel

Think little girls look absolutely gorgeous dressed in long white dresses, wings, and a halo? What a great costume to bring out their sweet and innocent side, perhaps to balance out a sibling dressed as a mischievous [devil](#16)!

## 16. Devil

For every sweet little [angel](#15) there has got to be a little devil! It takes a special kid to pull off a devil costume. Don’t worry, dressing your kid as a devil doesn’t mean you think he is devious. It’s just a fun and classic Halloween costume for kids.

## 17. Cowboy and Cowgirl

I remember dressing as a cowgirl one year for Halloween. I already owned a pair of cowboy boots, so putting together the rest of the costume was a cinch. Consider dressing up your little one as a cowboy or cowgirl too if they already have the boots.

## 18. Pirate

Thank you Jack Sparrow for bringing back all of the pirates for Halloween. I find pirate costumes to be so much fun and adorable on boys and girls. There are so many variations of this classic Halloween costume. You can stream his famous adventures on [Disney+](https://www.disneyplus.com) to get into character. You are sure to find one that is unique for your child.

Halloween is a time for fun and creativity. Even when choosing a classic Halloween costume for kids, you can use a little imagination and put your own spin on the idea. What are some ways you would update a classic Halloween costume? Are there any ideas you want to add to this list?
