13 The Kremlin

Via 15 Insane LEGO Creations
This might take a bit of time, but it sure looks cool, doesn't it?

***

The Kremlin is a fortified complex located in the heart of Moscow, Russia. It is the official residence of the President of the Russian Federation and a symbol of the Russian state. Built in the 15th century, the Kremlin has been a major political center for centuries and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site.

The Kremlin is made up of several cathedrals, palaces, and towers, such as the Ivan the Great Bell Tower, the Grand Kremlin Palace, and the Cathedral of the Assumption. Inside the complex are several museums, including the Kremlin Armory, the State Historical Museum, and the Diamond Fund.

The Kremlin complex is also home to the world's largest collection of Fabergé eggs, a series of jeweled eggs created by the House of Fabergé from 1885 to 1917. The eggs are a symbol of the wealth and power of the Russian royal family and the Russian Empire.

The Kremlin is also home to the world's largest bell, the Tsar Bell. The bell, which weighs 200 tons, was cast in 1733, but was damaged in a fire in 1737. It is now on display in the Ivan the Great Bell Tower.

A visit to the Kremlin is a must for anyone visiting Moscow.

21 Gothic Church

Via cathedral of Cologne
I love the color choices for this awe inspiring church.

***

The Gothic Church, also known as the Cathedral of Cologne, is a stunning architectural masterpiece that has been captivating people for centuries. This church is located in Cologne, Germany and is known for its intricate details, towering spires, and beautiful stained glass windows. It was built in the Gothic style, which was popular during the medieval period, and is characterized by its pointed arches, ribbed vaults, and flying buttresses. The church took over 600 years to complete and is now a UNESCO World Heritage Site. Its vibrant color choices, such as the use of red sandstone, make it stand out and add to its overall grandeur. It is not only a religious site but also a popular tourist attraction, drawing in visitors from all over the world.
