# 8 Engaging Activities to Do With Kids at Home or Outdoors

Kids have tons of energy, and sometimes keeping up with them requires a variety of engaging **activities** they will genuinely enjoy. Whether it's a holiday, a long weekend, or even just after school, there are great **activities to do with your kids** that will hold their interest and keep you entertained as well. Instead of just turning on the TV, sitting at the computer, or playing games on a smart phone, you can bond and make lasting memories. Here are 8 awesome activities to do with kids of all ages.

A quick look at some of the things you can easily do:

- Put on a show or performance

- Teach a new skill or craft

- Organize an outdoor scavenger hunt

## 1. Put on a Show

This is a highly entertaining **activity to do with your kids** where you can invite friends, neighbors, and other family members to join in. Everyone has a special talent, so why not put on a showcase featuring singers, dancers, actors, magicians, and any other talents your kids would love to share. You can put on silly outfits and hats, encourage role play, and even have the audience guess what animal or movie star you are trying to imitate! You can look up simple scripts on [Drama Notebook](https://www.dramanotebook.com/free-plays-for-kids/) to get started and spark their imagination.

## 2. Teach Them Something New

If mom or dad has a special craft or talent, what better activity to teach the kids so they can share and enjoy it too. Maybe mom loves to [scrapbook](https://allwomenstalk.com/top-blogs-on-scrapbooking/) or crochet, or perhaps dad knows how to play guitar or loves to cook. These are wonderful **activities to do with the kids** so you can pass valuable skills down to future generations of your family and remember that great grandma or great grandpa was the one who showed everyone how. It is a fantastic bonding experience that yields tangible results.

## 3. Go on a Scavenger Hunt

Put together two teams or more for a fun scavenger hunt that kids of all ages will enthusiastically enjoy. Make a list of silly things to find, like a green fire hydrant or someone wearing a brightly colored hat! You can also have your kids make their own list or help add items to the list mom and dad created. Take pictures of the things on your scavenger hunt so you will always remember the fun you had and that this is an **activity to do with your kids** again and again!

| Scavenger Hunt Item | Suggested Location | Points Value |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| Green fire hydrant | Neighborhood Walk | 10 Points |
| Purple flower or leaf | Local Park | 5 Points |
| Uniquely shaped rock | Backyard | 5 Points |

## 4. Re-purpose Something Old

This is a great **activity to do with the kids** that will teach them about recycling, reusing, and re-purposing in a highly creative way. Don't just throw out those old socks; wash them and make colorful sock puppets! You can also show them how to re-purpose old furniture, use odd items to [create](https://lifestyle.allwomenstalk.com/creative-ways-to-repurpose-an-old-door/) something unexpected, or turn empty glass jars into pretty painted vases. For inspiration, the [EPA's recycling guide](https://www.epa.gov/recycle) offers excellent tips on what materials can be easily saved and transformed.

## 5. Bake a Unique Masterpiece

Kids often feel like their parents are always telling them what to eat and what not to eat, so why not create an **activity to do with your kids** that will let them go wild in the kitchen! Tell your kids to go in the kitchen and pick eight fun food items like marshmallows, cookie dough, jelly beans, chocolate chips, peanut butter, and whatever else they can safely get their hands on. Have them mix it all together for a fun dessert night they will never forget! You can even incorporate [teaching them a new cooking skill](#2) while they assemble their crazy concoctions.

## 6. Bicycle Obstacle Course

Take your **kids** to the park or an empty school parking lot to set up this fun **activity** they will definitely want to invite their friends to. Use orange cones or cardboard boxes and homemade traffic signs for the course, and make little ribbons or awards to show them what a great job they did navigating the obstacles. Always remember, when putting together **activities to do with your kids** that include things like going to the park, using tools or sharp objects, and cooking in the kitchen, to teach your kids about parental supervision and safety first.

## 7. Write a Book Together

Kids have absolutely amazing imaginations and can create wild stories about cowboys in space, dinosaurs on Mars, or even a cereal-eating monster. Take their wonderful imaginations and turn them into an amazing **activity to do with kids** by writing a [book](https://parenting.allwomenstalk.com/books-for-your-childs-collection/) together and even drawing pictures to go along with the silly story. It helps develop their reading and writing skills while letting their creativity shine.

## 8. Volunteer

This will likely be the most rewarding **activity you do with your kids**, as not only will you be spending this time helping others, but you will also be showing your **kids** a selfless act of lending a hand. Whether you are [volunteering](https://allwomenstalk.com/7-places-you-should-volunteer-your-time-for/) at a local nursing home, cleaning up a park, walking dogs at an animal shelter, or simply visiting with a neighbor who lives alone, finding local opportunities through platforms like [VolunteerMatch](https://www.volunteermatch.org/) is a great start. Your child will see how happy this **activity** makes others, and you will both walk away with an immense sense of pride and a stronger connection to your community.
