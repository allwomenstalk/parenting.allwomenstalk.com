# 9 Beneficial Ways to Adjust to Single Parenthood ...

Whether you got a divorce or ended your relationship with your child's father, you benefit greatly by following the nine beneficial ways to adjust to single parenthood. This is a huge adjustment, and requires careful precision. But you can do this without acquiring gray hair or calling an exorcist. To get you started, here are the nine beneficial ways to adjust to single parenthood.

## 1. Balancing Discipline and Kindness

As a single parent, you become both mom and dad inside your home; this is why you should examine the ways to adjust to single parenthood. You must instill order amid the chaos, but also exhibit kindness. Divorces and separations are hard for children, too. Balance allows you to handle anxieties and worry while also preventing your children from taking advantage of you.

## 2. Reassessing Your Parenting Style

Although it may seem like second-nature to discuss your ex with your children, you should proceed with caution. It is never a good idea to bash them in front of your kids. You are not required to like your ex, but it is conducive to your child's well-being to refrain from displaying negativity. Alterations in parenting style may prove effective in this area.

## 3. Scheduling Family Time

Your finances may change drastically after a divorce. This could require you to work longer hours. If this is the case, you should make up a schedule for family time. It doesn't have to be anything extravagant, but it should be fused with ample quality time with your children.

## 4. The Buddy System

It is likely that you have at least one close friend that has become a single parent. She or he can help you with this adjustment, by giving you pointers. They can also offer you assistance when it comes to giving you a break. By initiating the buddy system, you can alternate weekend nights in which you or your friend enjoys a night out alone.

## 5. Play Dates

When you have small children, play dates are essential. This provides your child with the opportunity to make new friends, and it gives you time to talk to other adults. The life of a single parent is busy and hectic at times. A play date gives you a small break which allows you to recharge.

## 6. Re-evaluate Your Goals

Begin this step by making a list of your life goals. Now, adjust them based on the amount of time you have available. It doesn't sound easy, does it? I choose to attend college online. While ultimately you will pay more overall, the convenience is well worth it.

## 7. Proceed with Caution

Dating as a single mother is tough; you should proceed with caution. Your biggest ally is your gut instinct. If it doesn't feel right, it isn't. As I learned the hard way, there are plenty of fish in the sea, but you have to channel away from the sharks.

## 8. Your Teen Doesn't Hate You

Teens who experience a separation from a parent due to a divorce or the end of the relationship tend to lash out. Although it comes naturally, don't take it personally. Be there for them, as much as they will allow. Don't take any request for space as a personal attack. And remember, they are hurting, too.

## 9. Me Time

Everyone needs time to themselves. It is only natural. Although it may seem that you rarely have a moment to yourself - cherish all that you receive. Take advantage of this time to recharge your battery and relax. You've earned it.

Becoming a single parent is scary in the beginning. However, you can do this without losing your mind. What are some ways that you have coped with this major life change?
