# 9 Practical Ways to Keep Your Toddler Occupied When You Are Unwell

**All parents have been there:** whether you are unwell or just plain exhausted, you need creative ways to keep your toddler occupied. When you feel like you have run out of ideas, here are 9 tried-and-true methods to keep your toddler entertained while you rest or just need a moment to yourself.

## 1. Let Them Write a Letter

One of my favorite go-to activities is to grab a pad and pencil or open a document on the laptop and let them go to town. It does not have to be a "real" letter; their scribbles and typos are meaningful to them. When you are feeling better, take it a step further and mail it to someone special. Grandparents love anything their grandchild creates just for them, even if they have to decode the message themselves.

## 2. Have a Talent Show

While you are relaxing on the couch or in bed, play the role of the judge and let them showcase their best talents. You get to be entertained and rest at the same time. This simple engagement might even help you feel a little better.

## 3. Pretend Camping

Set up a tent inside the house and let the kids pretend they are camping. Have them bring their favorite stuffed animals or a few books to read. You get to be comfy with a blanket and pillow while they are "camping." If the weather permits, take the tent outdoors for some fresh air; the sunshine might give everyone a little boost of energy.

## 4. Allow Extra Screen Time

I know what you might be thinking, and I am not a big fan of using the TV as a babysitter. But when we are sick and just do not have the energy to be "super-parents," this can be a lifesaver. If screen time is usually a luxury, this is a surefire way to keep them entertained while you get the rest you need.

## 5. Use Educational Apps

**There are so many wonderful tools available today:** from interactive stories to simple counting games, there are plenty of toddler-friendly apps that are both fun and educational. Picking something engaging can turn screen time into a productive learning experience while you recover.

## 6. Create an Indoor Obstacle Course

Use pillows, cushions, and sturdy chairs to create a simple obstacle course. It is an excellent way for your little one to burn off energy safely indoors while you supervise from a comfortable spot.

## 7. Sensory Play With Dry Pasta

Give your toddler a large tray and some dried pasta or rice to sort or scoop. It is a fantastic sensory activity that keeps them focused and quiet for extended periods. Just be sure to supervise to keep things clean.

## 8. Read-Aloud Audiobooks

Sometimes you just want to close your eyes. Pop on an engaging children's audiobook or podcast. It keeps their imagination active and provides a calm, quiet atmosphere for the whole room.

## 9. A Simple Craft Project

Pull out some stickers, washable markers, and construction paper. Let them decorate to their heart's content. It is low-mess, high-engagement, and gives you plenty of time to recharge your batteries.
